### Prompt 2 — Error in Professional or Academic Setting (300 words)

**Story to Use:** Barrow consent patient, Barrow Neurological Institute, summer 2022 or 2025

**Framing Shift from PS:** In your PS, you center her terror as a *discovery about medicine*. For this prompt, lead with the professional error itself:

> *I left the room thinking I had handled a tissue-consent conversation correctly. Two days later, her nurse told me she had been terrified and had not heard most of what I said.*

**The Three Required Elements:**

1. **The error:** Treated her composure as agreement; optimized for protocol completion rather than patient understanding; got a valid signature without ensuring she was actually informed.

2. **Disclosure:** This is where you need to be direct. You learned about the error from the nurse — a passive disclosure that you then made active by going back to reflect and re-examine your approach. Consider whether you returned to the patient (if that's true) or spoke to your PI. Be honest; the learning from passive disclosure is still valid and honest.

3. **Systemic change:** You began asking "What are you most worried about?" before presenting the form. You started treating hesitation and composure differently. You carried this into ophtho work, ARMR Systems engineering questions, every clinical context since.

**Draft Opening:** *She laughed through most of our consent conversation, and I read the room as calm. Two days later, her nurse corrected me...*

**Draft Close:** *I now treat quietness the way I treat an anomalous data point: as something that requires investigation before it can be accepted.*
