## Voice Rules

Your strongest writing formula is:

1. One concrete scene.
2. One clear action you took.
3. One lesson stated without overexplaining.
4. One forward link to medicine or the school.

Do:

- Use specific nouns: "visual-field prompts," "drainage ditch," "silicone cover," "Dundalk porch."
- Own mistakes without melodrama.
- Keep the patient/community as the subject, not yourself as rescuer.
- Tie engineering to human use, not cleverness.
- Use school-specific facts only where they are real and connected.

Do not:

- Repeat primary language verbatim.
- Say "underserved" without showing the actual barrier.
- Overclaim Texas residency or border understanding.
- Use every value in a values prompt just because it is listed.
- Make ethics prompts sound like you are hunting for misconduct.
